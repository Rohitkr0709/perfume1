... What is Perfume? ...

The word perfume comes from the Latin phrase, “per” meaning “thorough” and “fumus” meaning “smoke”. The French later gave the name “parfum” to the smells produced by burning incense. Indeed, the first form of perfume was incense, first made by the Mesopotamians about 4000 years ago.

...How Is Perfume Made? ...

There are records dating back to the 2nd millennium BC describing a perfume maker who distilled flowers, oil, and calamus with other aromatics, then filtered and put them back in the still several times. Throughout the years while the process has modernised and has been produced on a much larger scale it is still very much following a similar path to those who started it. Humans have been using scents from nature such as fruits, flowers and spices to produce pleasant smelling fragrance for many years. Nowadays you will find fragrance in most items from soaps to lotions, candles and cleaning products and of course personal perfumes.
